Recommendation & Rationale

Strengths
Patient Flow First scores highest on patient impact (9/10) and operational feasibility (8/10). The initiative uses data already present in PAS, ED, bed management and discharge workflows, making it easier to deliver without waiting for a full clinical data platform uplift. It also addresses a visible operational pressure: ED crowding, bed availability, discharge delay and inpatient flow.
Conditions
Approval is conditional on: (1) agreement on patient flow definitions across ED, inpatient units and discharge teams; (2) confirmation of source-system ownership for PAS, ED and bed data; (3) privacy review for patient-level drill-through; (4) clinical operations sign-off before executive reporting is used for daily performance review.
Risks
The main risk is interpretation. Flow measures are sensitive to timestamp quality, late updates, ward transfer rules and local workflow differences. If the dashboard is used without clear definitions, teams may dispute the numbers rather than act on the bottlenecks. A data quality strip should be included in the first release.
Fallback
If patient flow data ownership cannot be agreed quickly, Elective Backlog should be the fallback. It has a clearer operating cohort, strong executive visibility and a narrower data footprint. Quality Reporting is strategically important, but it should follow once coding, clinical definitions and governance controls are stronger.
